About Me

I'm Lita, from Phnom Penh, Cambodia. I'm currently pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science, with a minor in Art History at Carleton College, MN, US.

I am passionate about using media and technology to provide underprivileged youth with better education and opportunities. I enjoy working in a collaborative culture, and taking initiative to solve challenging problems in my community and in society. I have experience working at start-ups, and non-profit organizations. I believe that I can and will create a positive change in society through my career in tech and passion for social entrepreneurship!

Semester at Sea

During my gap year, I traveled by ship to 11 countries and 4 continents, while taking college courses with Semester At Sea. I was able to engage with different cultures and people, and the experiences I had contributed greatly to my personal growth.

Semester at Sea

After graduating high school, I took a gap year and for the first 3-4 months, I got a full-ride scholarship to go to Semester at Sea, a life changing experience across 11 countries and 4 continents! I took 4 college level courses in geography, political science, economics development and finance. The best part about it was that I could apply what I learnt to the countries I visited, expanding my understanding of the world. Some of my memorable experiences are:

  • South Africa: Taking an independent trip to two informal settlements just at the outskirts of Cape Town, South Africa in which I learnt about the way the community functions - the council of people, the school and the wealth disparities between communities. I also met amazing strong women who helped me see so much more than just poverty - joy in living.
  • Ghana: Going to a floating village, Nzulenzu, Ghana where I learnt about the troubles, the joy and all the in-betweens. There, I made a promise to the chief that when I can help, I will go back and do my best to bring better health care and education to the people of Nzulenzu.
  • India: I loved Cochin, and the villages around it. I am happy I made the decision to not fly elsewhere within the five days I have in India and just stay in Cochin to explore the day-to-day lives of people. I was very lucky to have made a friend, Anub, a tuk-tuk driver/homestay ownwer who showed me around the fishing villages, his childhood home, his school, and Cochin's local arts and culture.

Check out my "Journey of a Lifetime" series blog at Medium for more!
